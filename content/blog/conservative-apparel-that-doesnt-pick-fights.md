There's a version of conservative apparel that seems designed primarily to irritate people.

The aggressive fonts. The slogans that read like they were written by someone who just got into an argument on the internet. The designs that are less about expressing a belief and more about provoking a reaction.

That's a choice. But it's not the only choice.

## A Different Kind of Conservative Shirt

There's a large population of Americans whose politics are genuinely conservative — who believe in limited government, personal responsibility, American exceptionalism, faith as a foundation — who have no interest whatsoever in wearing those beliefs as a provocation.

They've had the arguments. They've made their cases. They've watched the debates and arrived at their conclusions. Now they'd like a shirt that reflects who they are without turning every grocery store trip into an incident.

That's the audience these designs are built for.

The designs above don't hide what they believe. The patriotism is real. The faith is genuine. The values are sincere.

What they don't do is perform those beliefs for an audience that isn't interested. That's not timidity — it's the same confidence that characterizes the people who wear them well.

The people who get it will get it. The people who don't aren't the point.
